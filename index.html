Open Letter

To the Leadership of CERSAI

17 June 2026

The Chairperson / Chief General Manager

Central Registry of Securitisation Asset Reconstruction and Security Interest of India (CERSAI)

22nd Floor, HUDCO Vishala Building, Bhikaji Cama Place, New Delhi — 110 066

Subject: Voluntary Proposal for the Redesign of the Central KYC (CKYC) Card — Good-Faith Design Submission

Respected Sir / Madam,

I write to you with the deepest respect for the institution of CERSAI and its critical role in India's financial and legal infrastructure. The Central KYC Records Registry has been an important instrument in reducing redundancy across the Indian financial system, and the work CERSAI has done in building and operating this registry at scale deserves sincere appreciation.

I recently had occasion to review the existing CKYC card design — the physical identity document issued to citizens as evidence of their Central KYC registration. While I recognise the functional purpose the current design serves, I respectfully submit that, in my personal assessment, the existing card does not yet reflect the same level of visual quality, usability, and security-oriented design that citizens have come to expect from major Indian government identity documents, such as:

  • Aadhaar Card — a world-recognised model of clean, accessible government ID design
  • PAN Card — distinguished by clarity, hierarchy, and compact security features
  • Driving Licence — modern holographic and machine-readable design
  • Voter ID Card — recently refreshed with improved visual standards

Motivated entirely by a desire to contribute to the improvement of citizen-facing government services, I have voluntarily prepared a redesigned CKYC card — a proposal that I offer freely to CERSAI for consideration, adaptation, or implementation in any form CERSAI sees fit.

The proposed redesign aims to improve:

  • Visual hierarchy — so that the most critical information (KYC Identifier, cardholder name) is immediately visible at a glance
  • Readability — through consistent typography, generous field spacing, and high-contrast field labels
  • User experience — by presenting information in a logical, scannable layout that mirrors the citizen's mental model
  • Modern appearance — with clean geometry, rounded card boundaries, and a premium finish aligned with contemporary Indian government design standards
  • Security-oriented visual elements — including guilloche wave patterns, Ashoka Chakra-inspired watermarks, micro-line backgrounds, and security barcode strips
  • Alignment with the Indian national identity — through respectful use of the Tricolor palette (saffron, white, and green) and deep navy government-authority tones

This proposal is released under the Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International (CC BY 4.0) license. CERSAI, any department of the Government of India, or any authorised implementation partner is entirely free to use, modify, adapt, and implement this design without any fee or royalty. The only condition is that appropriate credit is given to the original creator.

I make no claim to authority over CERSAI's design decisions and understand that the actual card design involves considerations far beyond visual aesthetics — including regulatory requirements, printing technology, legal mandates, and security specifications. This proposal is intended purely as creative input and a demonstration of what is possible.

I hope this small contribution is received in the spirit in which it is offered — as a gesture of goodwill from a citizen who believes that great design and great governance are not mutually exclusive.

With warm regards and the highest respect for CERSAI's mission,

Sincerely,

Pt. Prashant Tripathi

Independent Software Engineer & Open-Source Contributor

Design Comparison

Existing vs. Proposed

Current Design

Existing CKYC Card

The current card uses a plain white background with standard system fonts and a two-panel layout. While functional, it lacks a defined visual hierarchy, security background elements, and the premium finish seen in comparable Indian government identity documents.

Proposed Redesign — Front

New CKYC Card (Front)

Deep navy header with the CERSAI logo, prominent KYC Identifier banner, clean data grid with tiered typography, photo zone with security corner brackets, guilloche background, and Tricolor strips fully clipped within rounded card boundaries.

Front Side — Full Detail

🏛️

Government Identity

Deep navy header, CERSAI logo, "Government of India" label, and Digital India badge establish official authority instantly.

🔢

KYC Identifier

The KYC number is given its own dedicated banner row in high-contrast gold on navy — impossible to miss at a glance.

📐

Tricolor Strips

Saffron, white, and green bands are drawn inside the card's clipPath, ensuring they never bleed past rounded corners.

🔐

Security Patterns

Guilloche sinusoidal waves, Ashoka Chakra radial watermark, micro-line diagonal fill, and a security barcode strip at the base.

🖋️

Typography Hierarchy

Three-level hierarchy: muted uppercase labels → semibold values → bold primary fields. Information is scannable in under two seconds.

📷

Photo Zone

Framed with a rounded clip and security corner brackets. Replace the SVG placeholder with an <image> tag for production.
